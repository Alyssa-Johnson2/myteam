Coach Nagy

Personal History

Matt Nagy was born April 24, 1978 in Dunellen, New Jersey. He is 6’2” and 220 pounds. He attended Manheim Central High School, Pennsylvania, where he played high school football and was the team’s star quarterback. After high school, he played college football at Delaware by the end of which he had recorded 8,214 career passing yards. Matt is happily married to his high school sweetheart Stacey with whom he has four children – all boys. Nagy told Chiefs website, that they tried for a girl but got twin boys instead. Their sons are Brayden, Tate, Jaxon, and Jett.

Matt Nagy

Professional History

Nagy is an American football coach and former quarterback who is the head coach of the Chicago Bears of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football for the University of Delaware, and subsequently was signed as a street free agent by the New York Dragons of the Arena Football League (AFL). He played for the Dragons, Carolina Cobras, Georgia Force and Columbus Destroyers of the AFL for six seasons before beginning his NFL coaching career.
